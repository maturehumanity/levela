# Citizenship and Verification Policy v0.1

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how a person moves from signup to verified status, citizenship, active citizenship, and governance eligibility inside Levela.

## 2. Verification Requirements

Verification may require:

- complete personal information submission
- complete contact information submission
- live in-app digital verification
- discrepancy review
- anti-duplication and legitimacy checks

Verification alone does not grant citizenship, active status, or governance rights.

## 3. Data Protection

Levela should protect sensitive identity data through:

- strong encryption at rest
- least-privilege access
- segmented storage
- auditable access handling
- off-chain storage of raw identity materials
- on-chain or public anchoring only for hashes, attestations, proofs, and revocation states where appropriate

## 4. Citizenship

A verified member may become a citizen by:

- remaining signed up for 30 days, or
- manually accepting citizenship after 14 days where review is complete and no blocking discrepancy exists

Citizenship means acceptance of Levela's internal civic framework, principles, rules, code of conduct, and related institutional procedures for participation inside the Levela system.

## 5. Active Status

Verified and citizen badges are distinct from active status. Before activation, a person may be a citizen but not yet active. Once the relevant activation threshold is recognized under Levela's constitutional framework, citizens within scope become active automatically.

## 6. Governance Eligibility

Governance eligibility requires more than citizenship. At minimum, Levela may require:

- verified status
- citizen status
- active status where activation applies
- minimum Levela score
- minimum time in good standing
- no serious unresolved sanctions
- trusted governance action path, including native mobile app requirements where adopted
- role-specific training or certification where applicable

## 7. Suspension and Revocation

Verification, active status, and governance eligibility may be suspended or revoked under appropriate procedures. Governance eligibility should generally be easier to suspend than citizenship itself, while still remaining reviewable and appealable.
