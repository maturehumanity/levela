# Levela Constitution v0.1

## Article 1. Foundational Principles

Levela is founded on legitimacy, truthfulness, distributed stewardship, privacy protection, auditable process, identity integrity, proportional trust, and peaceful civic participation.

## Article 2. Civic Belonging

Levela may recognize registered members, verified members, citizens, active citizens, and governance-eligible citizens. Each status carries different rights and duties and must not be confused with the others.

## Article 3. Rights and Duties

Participants are entitled to fair treatment, due process where applicable, and privacy within authorized limits. Participants must act honestly, avoid abuse, and respect the rules and institutions of the Levela system while participating in it.

## Article 4. Verification and Legitimacy

Rights that affect institutional legitimacy require stronger verification than ordinary participation. Identity systems should minimize centralized exposure of raw personal data while still enabling strong anti-fraud and anti-duplication controls.

## Article 5. Governance Eligibility

No person may hold governance permissions unless the constitutional and policy requirements for governance eligibility are satisfied. Minimum Levela score, verification, active status where applicable, good standing, and trusted governance paths are valid entry gates for governance rights.

## Article 6. Role Domains

Levela governance and stewardship should be organized by domain, including identity verification, moderation and conduct, governance and constitutional review, policy and legal review, treasury and finance oversight, market oversight, technical stewardship, dispute resolution, security and incident response, and civic education and certification.

## Article 7. Decentralization and Maturity

Founder stewardship exists only where a domain is still immature. Each domain must transition away from Founder operational responsibility once enough verified and authorized citizens exist to sustain it safely. Decentralization should occur domain by domain, not merely by aspiration.

## Article 8. Founder Office

The Founder Office is singular, personal, permanent as constitutional recognition, and limited as operational power. The Founder is the initiator of Levela and remains constitutionally protected, but not omnipotent. Founder status is protected; Founder action is bounded by scope and procedure.

## Article 9. Advisory and Constitutional Bodies

Levela may establish advisory, constitutional, ratification, and diplomatic bodies to preserve high-level legitimacy, review, and continuity without collapsing into one-person rule.

## Article 10. Decision Classes

Levela may distinguish among ordinary, elevated, constitutional, and emergency decisions. Each class should later receive specific thresholds, procedures, and review requirements.

## Article 11. Activation

Levela may recognize country-level or world-level activation under separate activation rules. Activation changes internal civic standing for participating Levela citizens inside the system and should be treated as an internal constitutional event, not as a casual product metric.

## Article 12. Integrity, Recovery, and Continuity

No core function should remain permanently dependent on a single person, single secret, or single operational path. Compromise, coercion, incapacity, abuse, and recovery scenarios must be anticipated and procedurally handled.

## Article 13. Interpretation

This Constitution must be interpreted to preserve legitimacy, decentralization, institutional maturity, privacy, verifiability, and continuity together. Where conflict appears, Levela should prefer interpretations that reduce concentration of unchecked power while preserving the system's founding direction.
