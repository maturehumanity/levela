## Article I: Foundational Principles

1. Human dignity is inviolable.
2. All persons are equal in rights and civic standing.
3. Freedom and responsibility are inseparable.
4. Governance exists to serve the public good.
5. Public power must be lawful, limited, transparent, and accountable.
6. Public systems must be designed for inclusion, safety, and resilience.
7. Future generations hold legitimate moral and civic claims on present decisions.
8. The commons, including natural and digital commons, must be protected from extraction and irreversible harm.
