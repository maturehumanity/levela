# Universal Constitution

Version: 0.1 (Foundational Draft)  
Status: Working draft for review and amendment  
Context: Levela civilization framework

---

## Preamble

We, the people, establish this Constitution to secure human dignity, protect freedom, ensure justice, preserve peace, steward the commons, and build a civilization that develops every person without exploitation.

We affirm that power is a trust, not a possession; that technology must serve humanity; and that prosperity is valid only when it is shared, sustainable, and grounded in responsibility.

This Constitution is universal in principle and practical in governance. It protects the individual, strengthens the community, and aligns institutions with long-term human and planetary flourishing.

---

## Article I: Foundational Principles

1. Human dignity is inviolable.
2. All persons are equal in rights and civic standing.
3. Freedom and responsibility are inseparable.
4. Governance exists to serve the public good.
5. Public power must be lawful, limited, transparent, and accountable.
6. Public systems must be designed for inclusion, safety, and resilience.
7. Future generations hold legitimate moral and civic claims on present decisions.
8. The commons, including natural and digital commons, must be protected from extraction and irreversible harm.

---

## Article II: Fundamental Rights

Every person is guaranteed the following rights, enforceable under law:

1. Right to life, bodily integrity, and personal security.
2. Right to equal protection under the law without discrimination.
3. Right to due process, fair hearing, and impartial adjudication.
4. Right to freedom of thought, conscience, expression, association, and peaceful assembly.
5. Right to privacy, including data privacy and protection from unlawful surveillance.
6. Right to identity, legal recognition, and secure civic participation.
7. Right to access essential conditions of human development: food, water, shelter, basic healthcare, and foundational education.
8. Right to meaningful work, fair compensation, and protection from coercive labor practices.
9. Right to participate in public decision-making.
10. Right to digital access and equal treatment in essential digital public services.
11. Right to explanation and appeal for materially significant automated decisions.
12. Right to a healthy environment and ecological protection.

No emergency, technology, or market interest may permanently suspend these rights.

---

## Article III: Civic Duties

All citizens and institutions share civic duties:

1. Respect the rights and dignity of others.
2. Contribute fairly to the common good through lawful civic, social, or economic participation.
3. Protect public institutions from corruption, capture, and abuse.
4. Preserve social trust through honesty in public processes.
5. Safeguard ecological systems and common resources.
6. Defend constitutional order against unlawful concentration of power.

---

## Article IV: Constitutional Governance

### 4.1 Separation of Functions

Public governance shall be organized into distinct and balanced functions:

1. Legislative function: enacts law and public policy.
2. Executive function: administers law within constitutional limits.
3. Judicial function: interprets law and protects rights.
4. Integrity and Audit function: independently verifies legality, finances, compliance, and public risk.

No function may absorb the core powers of another without constitutional amendment.

### 4.2 Subsidiarity and Scale

Decisions should be made at the most local level competent to solve the problem, with higher levels acting only when coordination, rights protection, or system stability requires it.

### 4.3 Democratic Legitimacy

1. Public authority originates from the people.
2. Major institutional offices must be selected through transparent and reviewable civic processes.
3. Term limits and conflict-of-interest restrictions must apply to high-power offices.
4. Governance records, voting logs, and policy rationales must be publicly available except where limited by lawful privacy or safety needs.

---

## Article V: Rule of Law and Justice

1. Laws must be public, stable, knowable, and equally applied.
2. Retroactive penal law is prohibited.
3. Collective punishment is prohibited.
4. Independent courts or equivalent tribunals must remain free from political and financial capture.
5. Every person has access to remedy for rights violations.
6. Public institutions are liable for unlawful harm caused by negligence, abuse, or unconstitutional action.

---

## Article VI: Economic Order and Public Value

1. The economy exists to support human flourishing, not extraction by a minority.
2. Market activity is legitimate when it is transparent, non-coercive, and aligned with constitutional rights.
3. Anti-monopoly and anti-corruption laws are mandatory constitutional instruments.
4. Essential systems, including money and payment infrastructure, must remain publicly accountable.
5. Public budgets must be transparent, auditable, and outcome-linked.
6. Monetary and financial policy must prioritize stability, affordability of essentials, and broad participation in productive life.
7. Governance power shall not be granted purely by wealth concentration.

### 6.1 Monetary Constitution Alignment (Levela)

Within Levela, the Luma system and the Levela Monetary Authority shall be governed by this Constitution and by subordinate monetary law.  
Constitutional-level monetary changes include:

1. alteration of monetary rights,
2. changes to wallet and identity protections,
3. structural changes to monetary governance authority.

These changes require enhanced voting thresholds, extended public notice, and independent constitutional review.

---

## Article VII: Technology, Data, and AI

1. Technology must remain subordinate to human rights and constitutional law.
2. AI may advise, simulate, monitor, and recommend, but may not hold sovereign authority.
3. High-impact automated systems require:
   - explicit legal basis,
   - documented objectives and constraints,
   - audit logs,
   - human override pathways,
   - appeal mechanisms for affected persons.
4. Mass surveillance without individualized lawful basis is prohibited.
5. Sensitive personal data must be minimized, secured, purpose-limited, and time-bounded.
6. Public-interest algorithms must undergo regular bias, safety, and performance audits.

---

## Article VIII: Environment and Intergenerational Stewardship

1. Ecological integrity is a constitutional good.
2. Public institutions must apply a prevention-first standard to irreversible environmental harm.
3. Carbon, biodiversity, water, and soil impacts must be measured and disclosed for major projects.
4. Restoration duties apply where public or licensed activity causes ecological damage.
5. Future generations are recognized as stakeholders in constitutional review of long-horizon decisions.

---

## Article IX: Peace, Security, and Emergency Powers

1. The primary purpose of security institutions is protection of life, rights, and constitutional order.
2. Emergency powers are temporary, necessary, and proportionate only.
3. Emergency declarations must include:
   - legal basis,
   - objective criteria,
   - scope limits,
   - expiration date,
   - independent oversight.
4. Core non-derogable rights remain protected at all times.
5. All emergency actions are automatically reviewable and publishable after operational risk has passed.

---

## Article X: Constitutional Institutions

The following institutions are constitutionally required:

1. Constitutional Court or equivalent constitutional review chamber.
2. Independent Electoral and Civic Process Commission.
3. Public Audit and Integrity Office.
4. Rights Ombuds Office with direct citizen complaint access.
5. Monetary Governance Authority (for systems operating constitutional money functions).
6. Public Data and Algorithmic Accountability Authority.

Each institution must publish annual reports and be subject to external review.

---

## Article XI: Amendment and Constitutional Protection

### 11.1 Amendment Process

Amendments require all of the following:

1. public publication of exact text for a defined minimum review period,
2. supermajority approval in the legislative constitutional process,
3. independent constitutional legality review,
4. direct civic ratification where required by constitutional law.

### 11.2 Eternity Clauses

No amendment may abolish:

1. human dignity,
2. equal legal standing,
3. basic due process,
4. prohibition of arbitrary rule,
5. constitutional accountability of public power.

### 11.3 Supremacy

This Constitution is the supreme legal authority. Any law or policy inconsistent with it is invalid to the extent of inconsistency.

---

## Article XII: Ratification and Transition

1. This Constitution takes effect upon ratification under defined civic procedures.
2. Existing institutions shall transition to constitutional compliance within a fixed implementation calendar.
3. Transitional law may exist only to operationalize constitutional guarantees, not to dilute them.
4. A first constitutional review conference shall be held within 24 months of ratification.
