## Article IV: Constitutional Governance

### 4.1 Separation of Functions

Public governance shall be organized into distinct and balanced functions:

1. Legislative function: enacts law and public policy.
2. Executive function: administers law within constitutional limits.
3. Judicial function: interprets law and protects rights.
4. Integrity and Audit function: independently verifies legality, finances, compliance, and public risk.

No function may absorb the core powers of another without constitutional amendment.

### 4.2 Subsidiarity and Scale

Decisions should be made at the most local level competent to solve the problem, with higher levels acting only when coordination, rights protection, or system stability requires it.

### 4.3 Democratic Legitimacy

1. Public authority originates from the people.
2. Major institutional offices must be selected through transparent and reviewable civic processes.
3. Term limits and conflict-of-interest restrictions must apply to high-power offices.
4. Governance records, voting logs, and policy rationales must be publicly available except where limited by lawful privacy or safety needs.
