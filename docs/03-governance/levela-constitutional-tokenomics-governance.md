# Levela Constitutional Tokenomics + Governance Model

Version: Draft 1.0  
Project: Levela  
Status: Foundational Governance Draft

---

# Purpose

This document defines the foundational tokenomics, governance, treasury, and civic incentive framework for Levela.

Levela is intended as a decentralized, open-source ecosystem designed to help humanity coordinate fairly, transparently, intelligently, and peacefully.

Its economic and governance systems must reward:

- contribution
- responsibility
- long-term thinking
- real value creation
- citizen participation
- ethical conduct
- transparent stewardship

It must discourage:

- corruption
- manipulation
- wealth capture
- fake participation
- speculation without contribution
- abuse of centralized control

---

# I. Native Unit Structure

## Monetary Unit: Luma

Luma is the native transferable economic unit of Levela.

Primary use cases:

- exchange of goods and services
- ecosystem transactions
- treasury accounting
- grants and funding
- staking mechanisms
- rewards and incentives

## Civic Unit: Level Score

Level Score is a non-transferable civic reputation layer.

It cannot be bought, sold, transferred, inherited, or manipulated through wealth alone.

Level Score may be earned through verified positive contribution, such as:

- education
- useful work
- innovation
- mentorship
- public service
- ethical participation
- long-term consistency
- constructive governance involvement

This separation ensures wealth does not automatically equal influence.

---

# II. Monetary Supply Model

## Controlled Expanding Supply

Luma should not use a rigid hard-cap model.

A civilization-scale economy requires adaptive monetary policy capable of responding to real conditions.

Annual issuance may be tied to:

- verified population growth
- productive ecosystem activity
- treasury health
- demand expansion
- public infrastructure needs

## Suggested Inflation Targets

Normal operating conditions:

- 1% to 3% annual issuance growth

Emergency expansion:

- allowed only through constitutional governance approval

Deflationary burns:

- may occur through fees, penalties, or treasury policy when appropriate

Initial ecosystem allocation should be transparent and publicly auditable.

## Suggested Allocation

- development

Used for:

# IV. Governance Power Formula

Voting rights should not be based solely on token ownership.

## Recommended Hybrid Formula

Governance Weight =

- 40% Level Score
- 30% Time-Locked Luma Stake
- 20% Verified Participation History
- 10% Anti-Concentration Adjustment

## Intended Results

- wise active citizens matter
- long-term aligned holders matter
- large holders are moderated
- passive wealth cannot dominate governance

---

# V. Treasury Governance

Treasury assets belong to the ecosystem and are managed under constitutional rules.

## Treasury Spending Categories

- software development
- audits
- security
- education
- humanitarian initiatives
- research
- contributor grants
- local community chapters
- dispute resolution
- ecosystem growth

## Spending Controls

Large allocations should require:

- quorum
- public review period
- transparent proposal history
- majority or supermajority approval depending on size
- full on-chain or public ledger auditability

---

# VI. Founder Compensation Framework

Levela recognizes the extraordinary role of founders and early builders.

## Founder Compensation Sources

### 1. Genesis Allocation

Publicly disclosed and vested over time.

### 2. Steward Compensation

If actively serving the ecosystem in leadership or execution roles, founders may receive community-approved compensation.

### 3. No Permanent Control

Economic reward does not equal perpetual governance authority.

Founder governance privileges should sunset unless renewed through legitimate governance processes.

---

# VII. Anti-Corruption Protections

## No Hidden Control

Any governance-significant holdings must be transparently disclosed.

## Whale Resistance

Voting influence may taper after concentration thresholds.

## Fraud Penalties

Following due process, proven abuse may trigger:

- temporary suspension
- governance restrictions
- treasury recovery actions
- reputation penalties

---

# VIII. Citizen Reward Layer

A portion of issuance may reward verified positive contribution.

Examples:

- teaching skills
- mentoring
- volunteering
- environmental service
- innovation
- dispute mediation
- civic participation
- verified helpful work

This creates an incentive economy aligned with social good.

---

# IX. Monetary Stability Layer

Treasury reserves may include diversified holdings such as:

- fiat currencies
- productive assets
- stable instruments
- ecosystem revenues

## Decision Model

AI systems may recommend actions.

Human governance makes final decisions.

Principle:

AI advises. Humans decide.

# X. Multi-Chamber Governance Model

## Chamber A: Citizens

General participant body.

## Chamber B: Contributors

Builders, maintainers, domain experts.

## Chamber C: Constitutional Guardians

Protect rights, continuity, and anti-capture safeguards.

## Major Constitutional Changes

Require approval from at least two of three chambers.
