# Role Domains and Maturity Thresholds v0.1

## 1. Principle

Founder stewardship ends by domain, not all at once. A domain is mature only when enough verified and authorized people exist to sustain it safely without Founder operational dependence.

## 2. Universal Governance Baseline

No person should hold governance responsibility without:

- verified status
- minimum Levela score
- minimum good-standing period
- no serious active sanctions
- trusted governance action path
- any role-specific training or certification

## 3. Core Domains

The initial governance and stewardship domains are:

- identity verification
- moderation and conduct
- governance and constitutional review
- policy and legal drafting
- treasury and finance oversight
- market oversight
- technical stewardship
- dispute resolution and appeals
- security and incident response
- civic education and certification
- country or world activation oversight

## 4. Suggested First Maturity Thresholds

- Identity verification: 5 verifiers, 2 review leads, 2 appeals reviewers
- Moderation and conduct: 7 moderators, 3 senior moderators, 3 appeals reviewers
- Governance and constitutional review: 9 governance members, 5 constitutional reviewers, 5 ratification board members
- Technical stewardship: 5 technical stewards, 2 release stewards, 2 infrastructure reviewers
- Civic education and certification: 5 educators or certification reviewers, 2 examination reviewers

## 5. Founder After Maturity

Once a domain matures, Founder should no longer serve as the ordinary operational owner of that domain. Founder may still retain constitutional standing, advisory participation, and integrity-review access consistent with the Founder Role Charter.
