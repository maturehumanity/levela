# Founder Role Charter v0.2

## Article 1. Nature of the Office

The Founder is the initiator of Levela and the original source of its creation, direction, and early stewardship. The Founder Office is permanent as constitutional recognition, but not unlimited as operational power.

## Article 2. Identity

The Founder Office is singular, personal, unique, non-transferable, non-hereditary, and non-elective. Respect toward descendants may exist culturally, but creates no constitutional authority.

## Article 3. Public Standing

The Founder shall be visibly recognized throughout the application as the Initiator of the system and as a participant within it. The Founder should retain a credible and decent baseline standing that reflects the initiating role without implying universal expertise, infallibility, or omnipotence.

## Article 4. Permanent Rights

The Founder permanently retains:

- recognition as Founder
- standing to initiate constitutional review
- standing to raise system-integrity concerns
- standing to access governance and audit channels necessary for Founder duties
- standing to participate in future constitutional, advisory, and diplomatic bodies

## Article 5. Limits

The Founder does not possess absolute control over Levela. The Founder may not unilaterally pause decisions and may not permanently dominate ordinary governance after institutional maturity is reached. The Founder has no default right to private communications, financial privacy data, or sensitive personal records except where procedure, consent, or necessity allows.

## Article 6. Bootstrap Stewardship

Before institutional maturity, the Founder may temporarily carry responsibilities that later belong to verified and authorized role-holders. This exists because the system is immature, not because the Founder is superior in all matters.

## Article 7. Transition by Domain

Founder operational responsibility must shrink by function as the system matures. Domains should transition separately once enough verified and authorized citizens exist to sustain them safely.

## Article 8. Access

The Founder may access only what is necessary to fulfill Founder duties and temporary stewardship functions. General governance state, test environments, and limited user identity context may be visible where operationally required. Private communications, detailed personal records, and financial privacy data are excluded by default and should only be accessed where procedure expressly permits it.

## Article 9. Protected Status, Limited Action

Founder status is constitutionally protected and not casually overruled. This protection applies to office and standing, not to unlimited operational override.

## Article 10. Mature System Role

In a mature system, the Founder should function primarily as constitutional initiator, civic guarantor, senior advisory participant, and diplomatic-level figure within Levela rather than as ordinary operator of every domain.

## Article 11. Absence and Recovery

If Founder participation is required for a process and the Founder is unavailable beyond the allowed period, the Founder requirement lapses. No critical path may remain indefinitely blocked by Founder absence. Compromise, coercion, incapacity, and misuse must be anticipated through attributable actions, recovery procedures, and fallback paths.
